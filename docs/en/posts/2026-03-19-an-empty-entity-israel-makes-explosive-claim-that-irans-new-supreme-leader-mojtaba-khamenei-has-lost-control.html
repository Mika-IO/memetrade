"An EMPTY Entity!" Israel Makes EXPLOSIVE Claim That Iran's New Supreme Leader Mojtaba Khamenei Has LOST CONTROL

Israel Drops a Bombshell About Iran's Leadership

In a stunning intelligence assessment that has sent shockwaves through diplomatic circles, Israeli officials have publicly claimed that Iran's new Supreme Leader, Mojtaba Khamenei, is effectively an "empty entity" who has lost meaningful control over the country's military and political apparatus.

What Israel Is Alleging

The claim, which surfaced through Israeli defense briefings on March 19, 2026, suggests that the transition of power following the senior Khamenei's declining health has not gone smoothly. According to Israeli intelligence:

  • Fractured Command: Multiple power centers within the Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC) are operating with increasing autonomy, making decisions about military strikes and retaliatory actions without centralized approval.
  • Legitimacy Crisis: Unlike his father, Mojtaba allegedly lacks the religious and political authority to command unified obedience from Iran's sprawling security establishment.
  • Dangerous Unpredictability: A leader who cannot control his own military is, paradoxically, more dangerous than one who can — because no single decision-maker can de-escalate.

Why This Matters Right Now

The allegation comes at the worst possible time, as the Middle East conflict has escalated to include strikes on energy infrastructure across the entire Persian Gulf region. If Israel's assessment is correct, it means there may be no single person in Iran capable of ordering a ceasefire — even if one were strategically desirable.

The Information War Dimension

Critics caution that Israel has a strategic interest in portraying Iranian leadership as weak and fractured. Whether the intelligence is accurate or is itself a form of psychological warfare designed to demoralize Tehran remains hotly debated among analysts.

Regardless of the truth, the claim itself has become a major viral talking point, shaping how the world understands the conflict's trajectory.
